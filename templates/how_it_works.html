Best Practices

Writing Good Questions

  • Be specific: "What time should we meet on Friday?" is better than "When should we meet?"
  • Stay neutral: Avoid leading questions that suggest a preferred answer
  • Keep it simple: One question per poll works best

Creating Effective Options

  • Cover all choices: Make sure all reasonable options are included
  • Be mutually exclusive: Options shouldn't overlap
  • Use parallel structure: Keep options similar in format
  • Consider "Other": For open-ended questions, consider adding an "Other" option

Maximizing Response Rates

  • Share at the right time: Consider when your audience is most active
  • Provide context: Tell people why their vote matters
  • Set appropriate deadlines: Give people enough time to respond
  • Send reminders: A gentle reminder can boost participation

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I edit my poll after creating it?

For integrity reasons, polls cannot be edited after votes have been cast. We recommend previewing your poll carefully before sharing.

How do I delete my poll?

If you created the poll on this device/browser, you'll see a "Delete Poll" button. This uses a creator token stored in your browser session.

Can someone vote multiple times?

We use browser sessions to prevent duplicate votes. However, someone using a different browser or device could vote again. For high-stakes polls, consider other verification methods.

Are votes really anonymous?

Yes! We don't collect IP addresses, email addresses, or any identifying information. We only store a hashed token to prevent duplicate votes.

What happens when a poll expires?

Expired polls stop accepting new votes but remain viewable. They are automatically deleted from our servers after a short period.

Is there a limit to how many polls I can create?

To prevent abuse, we limit poll creation to 10 polls per hour per session. This should be plenty for normal use.

Can I see who voted for what?

No. Polls are completely anonymous. Even as the poll creator, you can only see aggregate vote counts, not individual votes.
